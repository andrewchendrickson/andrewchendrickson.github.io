#_ whois Andy Hendrickson

I currently serve as the Chief Technical Officer for the Enforcement Bureau at the Federal Communications Commission (FCC), where I provide strategic and technical leadership to support the agency’s mission of protecting the public interest. Focus areas include networks, the space economy, artificial intelligence, robocall and robotext mitigation, privacy, data protection, cybersecurity, spectrum enforcement, fraud prevention, mediation, and enforcement related to wireless and wireline network outages, 911 systems, public safety, critical infrastructure, homeland security, and national security.

Prior to joining the FCC, I spent over 20 years in the telecommunications industry as a technology executive. During my tenure at Verizon, I played a key role in the rollout of the 5G network and helped lead the engineering and operations of the Verizon Cloud Platform (VCP). I have managed and contributed to complex initiatives in software-defined networking (SDN), virtualization and cloud computing, cybersecurity, data analytics, and geographic information systems (GIS).

Personally, I am invested in open technology. I am involved with the Linux Foundation, Open Infrastructure Foundation, and the Network Time Foundation. I also participate on the Board of the Rutgers School of Communication & Information Alumni Association. My most significant growth always seems to come from the lessons of my two children. In my downtime, I'm either deep in the world of audio recording and engineering or rocking out on various instruments.

I hold dual degrees from Rutgers University, having studied at both the School of Environmental and Biological Sciences and the School of Communication and Information.
